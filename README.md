# Terrain Mapping and Topographic Analysis – Trishuli Hydropower Project

A scaled, physical topographic model of the Trishuli Hydropower Project (Trishuli Bazar, Nuwakot, Nepal), built using GIS-based terrain and contour mapping techniques to visualize the site's landforms and hydropower infrastructure.

> 📌 **Note:** This is a physical/hands-on engineering model, not a software project. This repository serves as a digital record of the design process, methodology, and outcomes, illustrated through the project report (PDF) and photographs of the constructed model.

---

## 📖 Overview

Trishuli Hydropower Station is a run-of-river plant situated on the banks of the Trishuli River. This project translates its topographic map into a physical, scaled-down model — cutting and layering individual contour lines to recreate the site's elevation profile, then marking key hydropower components (weir, penstock, anchor block, powerhouse) on the terrain using site coordinates and Google Earth references.

**Institution:** Kathmandu University, Department of Civil Engineering
**Supervisor:** Er. Santosh Chaudhary

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Determine the position of site features in terms of latitude, longitude, and altitude, and identify typical landform patterns
- Construct a demonstrative model of the topography of the Trishuli Hydropower Project
- Apply and understand the concepts of reduced scale and distorted scale in terrain modeling

---

## 🛠️ Methodology

1. **Preliminary Study** — Research on civil construction references and site topography
2. **Literature Review** — Study of run-of-river hydropower components and principles
3. **Consultation** — Guidance from supervisors and seniors
4. **Data Collection & Calculation** — Extraction of contour data and scale (R.F.) calculations from the topographic map
5. **Material Collection** — Sourcing of plywood, polypropylene foam, enamel, and adhesives
6. **Model Construction** — Layering contour cutouts to build elevation; painting and finishing
7. **Conclusion** — Verification of dimensions and final presentation

## 📐 Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Actual Value | Model Value | R.F. (Scale) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contour Interval | 80 m | 0.04 m | 1:2000 |
| Height of Highest Hill | 560 m | 0.28 m | 1:2000 |
| Length of Map | 5,700 m | 1 m | 1:5700 |
| Breadth of Map | 5,200 m | 1 m | 1:5200 |

**Additional site data:**
- Total contours: 7 (80 m each)
- River elevation from sea level: 500 m
- Highest point elevation from sea level: 1,060 m
- Approximate river length modeled: 6,940 m
- Area of selected region: 29.64 km²
- Coordinates: 27°55'09"–27°58'25" N, 85°08'45"–85°11'11" E

## 🧰 Tools & Materials

- **Reference/Mapping:** Topographic map (contour lines), Google Earth
- **Physical Materials:** Plywood (base), polypropylene foam (terrain), enamel paint, adhesive, wood dust
- **Techniques:** Reduced scale & distorted scale modeling, contour layering

---

## ✅ Results

- Successfully constructed a demonstrative, to-scale model of the Trishuli Hydropower Project's topography
- Gained hands-on familiarity with reduced and distorted scale modeling
- Applied concepts of latitude, longitude, and altitude to identify and mark real landform and infrastructure positions
